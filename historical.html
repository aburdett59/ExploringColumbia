Historical Sites

Columbia’s history stretches back centuries, and its heritage can be seen in the architecture, monuments, and museums that dot the city. Below are just a few of the most notable historical sites you can explore.

South Carolina State House

Completed in 1907, the State House is the center of government in South Carolina. The building’s distinctive architecture, grand interior, and surrounding monuments tell the story of the state’s past.

Address: 1100 Gervais St, Columbia, SC 29208

Hampton-Preston Mansion & Gardens

Built in 1818, this antebellum mansion offers a glimpse into Columbia’s 19th-century life. The property features carefully restored gardens and rotating exhibits that highlight the city’s cultural heritage.

Address: 1615 Blanding St, Columbia, SC 29201

Robert Mills House & Gardens

Designed by Robert Mills, the first American-born professional architect, this classical-style home dates back to the early 1800s. Guided tours showcase period furnishings, local history, and the story of the home’s design.

Address: 1616 Blanding St, Columbia, SC 29201

Columbia Museum of Art (Historic Collection)

Although the Columbia Museum of Art focuses on world-class art exhibits, it also houses historical collections that shed light on the region’s artistic and cultural development. Don’t miss the museum’s special exhibitions dedicated to South Carolina’s past.

Address: 1515 Main St, Columbia, SC 29201

Local Heritage Trails

For those who prefer the outdoors, Columbia’s heritage trails wind through neighborhoods and historic districts. Each trail features markers with facts about local landmarks, influential figures, and significant events that shaped the city.
